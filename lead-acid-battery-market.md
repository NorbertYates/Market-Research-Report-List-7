<p><p>Lead acid batteries are rechargeable energy storage devices that have been widely used for various applications, including automotive, industrial, and backup power systems. These batteries consist of lead dioxide as the positive electrode, sponge lead as the negative electrode, and sulfuric acid as the electrolyte. Their advantages include low cost, high reliability, and recyclability, making them a preferred choice for many industries.</p><p>The Lead Acid Battery Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.9% during the forecast period, driven by increased demand in automotive applications, particularly for electric and hybrid vehicles. The rise in renewable energy installations also contributes to market growth, as lead acid batteries are commonly used in off-grid solar systems for energy storage. </p><p>Recent trends indicate a shift towards enhanced battery technologies, such as absorbed glass mat (AGM) and gel-based lead acid batteries, which offer improved performance and safety. Additionally, advancements in recycling processes are boosting sustainability efforts in the industry. The growing need for uninterrupted power supply in various sectors further supports the overall expansion of the lead acid battery market, making it a vital component in the global energy landscape.</p></p>

<p><p>The lead-acid battery market comprises various types, including Valve Regulated Lead-Acid (VRLA) and flooded batteries. VRLA batteries are sealed and maintenance-free, ideal for applications where space and leakage are concerns. Flooded batteries, on the other hand, are vented and require regular maintenance, offering high performance and longevity, making them suitable for industrial uses. Other types include absorbed glass mat (AGM) and gel batteries, each catering to specific needs in renewable energy, automotive, and backup power solutions.</p></p>
