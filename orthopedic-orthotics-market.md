<p><p>The orthopedic orthotics market is divided into three main types. Upper-limb orthoses support and stabilize the arms and hands, aiding recovery from injuries or conditions like carpal tunnel syndrome. Lower-limb orthoses encompass devices for the feet and legs, helping with mobility issues due to conditions such as stroke or arthritis. Spinal orthoses are designed for the back, providing support for conditions like scoliosis or post-operative recovery. Each type plays a crucial role in enhancing mobility and overall quality of life.</p></p>
