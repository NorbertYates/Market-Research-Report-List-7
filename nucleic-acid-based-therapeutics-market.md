<p><p>The nucleic acid-based therapeutics market encompasses various strategies for targeting genetic material. Anti-sense and anti-gene therapies inhibit gene expression, while short inhibitory sequences (siRNAs) and ribozymes disrupt specific RNA functions. Gene transfer therapy involves delivering therapeutic genes to correct genetic disorders. Nucleoside analogs mimic natural nucleotides, interfering with nucleic acid synthesis. Aptamers are RNA or DNA molecules that bind specific targets, offering potential for precise treatment modalities. These approaches highlight innovative solutions in molecular medicine.</p></p>
