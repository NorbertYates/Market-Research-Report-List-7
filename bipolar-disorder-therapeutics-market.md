Antipsychotic drugs, like quetiapine, manage severe symptoms during manic or depressive phases. Antidepressant drugs, including SSRIs, address depressive episodes but require careful monitoring due to potential triggers for mania. Other drugs may include anxiolytics or anticonvulsants that support overall management. Collectively, these treatments aim to stabilize mood and improve daily functioning in individuals with bipolar disorder.</p></p>
