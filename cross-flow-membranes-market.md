<p><p>Cross-flow membranes are filtration systems that offer enhanced separation and concentration of substances in various liquid applications. They function by allowing the feed solution to flow parallel to the membrane surface, which minimizes fouling and allows for continuous filtration processes. <p><p>The Cross-Flow Membranes market includes several types based on filtration capabilities. Microfiltration membranes have large pore sizes, effectively removing suspended solids and bacteria. Ultrafiltration membranes have smaller pores, filtering out viruses and larger molecules. Nanofiltration membranes possess even finer pores, selectively allowing certain ions and small organic molecules to pass through while rejecting larger substances. Additionally, the "Other" market category encompasses specialized membranes or hybrid technologies that cater to niche applications, enhancing the versatility of cross-flow filtration processes.</p></p>
