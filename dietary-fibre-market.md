<p><ul><li>Water Soluble Dietary Fiber</li><li>Water Insoluble Dietary Fiber</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dietary fiber market is mainly categorized into two types: water-soluble and water-insoluble dietary fibers. Water-soluble dietary fiber dissolves in water, forming a gel-like substance, which aids in lowering cholesterol and improving glucose levels. Common sources include oats and beans. In contrast, water-insoluble dietary fiber does not dissolve in water and helps add bulk to the stool, promoting digestive health. Sources include whole grains and vegetables. Both types play crucial roles in overall health and wellness.</p></p>
