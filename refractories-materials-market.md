<p><ul><li>Acidic</li><li>Neutral</li><li>Basic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Refractory materials are categorized into acidic, neutral, and basic types based on their chemical resistance to slag and furnace environments. Acidic refractories, such as silica, resist acidic slags but can be attacked by alkaline materials. Neutral refractories, such as chromite, maintain stability with both acidic and basic environments. Basic refractories, like magnesia, withstand high temperatures and resist basic slags, making them suitable for steelmaking and non-ferrous industries, highlighting their importance in high-temperature applications.</p></p>
