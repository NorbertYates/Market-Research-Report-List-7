<p><p>The electroceuticals medicine market consists of two main types: implantable and non-invasive devices. Implantable electroceutical devices, such as pacemakers and neurostimulators, are surgically placed in the body to treat chronic conditions through electrical signals. Conversely, non-invasive electroceutical devices, like transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS) units, provide therapeutic effects without surgery, using surface electrodes to deliver electrical impulses. Both types aim to manage and alleviate various medical conditions, enhancing patient outcomes and quality of life.</p></p>
