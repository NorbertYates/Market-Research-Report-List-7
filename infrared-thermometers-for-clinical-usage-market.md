<p><p>Infrared thermometers for clinical usage are categorized into non-contact and contact types. Non-contact thermometers measure temperature from a distance, utilizing infrared technology to detect body heat without physical contact, promoting hygiene and comfort. In contrast, contact thermometers require direct placement on the skin or within body cavities to provide precise temperature readings. Both types are widely used in healthcare settings, each offering unique advantages based on the required application, speed of measurement, and patient interaction.</p></p>
