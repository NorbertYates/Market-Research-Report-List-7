Metal strain gauge sensors, made from materials like constantan or foil, are known for their stability and ease of use in various applications, including structural monitoring. Semiconductor strain gauge sensors, on the other hand, utilize advanced materials that offer higher sensitivity and are suitable for precision measurements, particularly in miniaturized systems. Both types cater to distinct needs in industries such as aerospace, automotive, and civil engineering.</p></p>
