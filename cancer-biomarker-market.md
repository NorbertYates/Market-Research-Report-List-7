<p><p>The cancer biomarker market comprises various types, notably protein biomarkers and genetic biomarkers. Protein biomarkers are specific proteins produced by cancerous tissues or cells, serving as indicators for diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment response. Genetic biomarkers, on the other hand, involve alterations in DNA sequences, such as mutations or epigenetic changes, that can signal the presence of cancer or predict therapy outcomes. Together, these biomarkers enhance personalized medicine approaches, improving detection, monitoring, and therapeutic strategies in cancer care.</p></p>
